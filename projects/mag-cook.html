Project 18

Mag-Cook

A self-contained heating pot that works like an induction cooker — but generates its own alternating current with spinning magnets instead of mains electricity.

Overview

Boiling water with magnets

Mag-Cook is a self-contained heating pot for water, food and drink. It works much like an induction cooker — using an alternating current to create an electromagnetic field that resonates in a metal pot to produce heat — except it needs no electricity. Instead, magnets generate the alternating current themselves.

How it works

Pull the cord, spin the magnets

As the cord is pulled, a cylinder filled with magnets in alternating polarity spins fast enough to heat the inner metal tube. The goal was an off-grid device that could boil water with no power supply at all.

The two challenges

Feasibility, then speed

First, whether induction was even achievable this way: to find out, we mounted a wheel of magnets on a lathe and measured how fast the tube would need to spin to reach 100°C. Second, how to hit that speed by hand: the gearing that multiplies the torque from the ratchet was borrowed from a chainsaw pull-cord and adapted to our design.
