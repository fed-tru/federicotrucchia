Overview

Sensing, beyond the instrument

Sensei is a wireless controller for AR/VR, developed for Roli’s R&D and funded by the Innovate UK program. The aim was to explore the potential of the SEA Interface and other in-house technologies beyond musical instruments — and to find new market opportunities.

Timeline

Kicked off, paused, resumed

Sensei was first kicked off in 2013, then paused to prioritise development of the Seaboard Grand, and resumed at the start of 2016. Over the following ten months I led the project and managed the budget and documents for the Innovate UK funding grant, while designing the prototype.

To keep development as lean as possible, everything was built on Arduino — which let us quickly add or remove functionality while evaluating different kinds of interaction.

Sensing

Tessellating the interface in 3D

We also used the project to test new concepts for our proprietary pressure sensor: specifically, hybrid flex PCBs capable of tessellating the Interface across complex three-dimensional objects.

Afterlife

From controller to spatial audio

We eventually repurposed the prototype: from an AR/VR controller into an experimental tool for playing with spatial audio.
