Overview

Sensing, beyond the instrument

Sensei is a prototype wireless controller for AR/VR, developed for Roli’s R&D and funded by the Innovate UK program. The aim was to explore the potential of the SEA Interface and other in-house technologies beyond musical instruments — and to find new market opportunities. I led the project, managed the relationship with Innovate UK, and designed the UX story and demo.

Approach

Fast, flexible, Arduino-first

To keep development fast and flexible, the entire prototype was built around Arduino-based hardware. That let us rapidly test interaction models, sensor configurations, and physical forms while continuously refining the experience.

The project also became a testbed for new pressure-sensing technologies, including flexible-PCB concepts capable of wrapping around complex three-dimensional surfaces.

Afterlife

From controller to spatial audio

We eventually repurposed the prototype: from an AR/VR controller into an experimental tool for playing with spatial audio.
