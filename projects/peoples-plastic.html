Project 16

The People’s Plastic

A design-research project into how Londoners access water on the go — and what it would take to stop them reaching for a plastic bottle.

Place
Side hustle
Role
Team member
Years
2018
Type
Speculative · design research
Location
London
Overview

Making plastic the awkward choice

An average Londoner gets through about 175 plastic water bottles a year. They’re cheap, convenient and everywhere — and when thirst takes hold on the go, we rarely stop to think about the environment. We glare at smokers in the street and carry a ‘bag for life’ to the shops, yet someone clutching a plastic bottle still reads as cool. This project set out to understand what it would take for people not to reach for one.

Brief

Investigating spontaneous behaviour

Our team’s brief was to investigate Londoners’ spontaneous behaviour: how do people access water when they’re out and about, and how can we cut the consumption of bottled water? We started from speculative-design techniques — looking at deterrents and incentives already used in other contexts, and how those measures might play out here.

Outcomes

Three speculative nudges

The outcomes imagine a future where public fountains are surrounded by aromatic herbs to improve the water’s taste; a redesigned label for plastic bottles in the same spirit as cigarette packaging, showing the consequences of disposable plastic; and a speculative National Geographic cover announcing the extinction of the blue whale.
