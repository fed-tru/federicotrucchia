Project 14

Open Oxy

A small wireless device that helps doctors monitor patients remotely — sensing heart rate, oxygenation and temperature, and streaming the data to the cloud so medics can track many patients at once.

Overview

Readings, streamed to the cloud

Open Oxy is a small wireless device that helps doctors in remote-monitoring situations. It embeds a pulse oximeter sensing heart rate, oxygenation and temperature — but instead of showing the readings locally, it streams them to an AWS server, where medics can track multiple devices and patients at once.

Why

A doctor friend, mid-pandemic

The idea came from a conversation with a friend serving as a doctor in a COVID-19 emergency response unit. Pulse oximeters had quickly become key to monitoring non-hospitalised patients: by calling each day to ask for their readings, medics could look after them while keeping precious hospital beds free for those who needed them most.

At peak times, though, that manual process ate a considerable amount of doctors’ time. Automating the reading and aggregation could save time, reduce stress, improve care for non-hospitalised patients — and give public-health institutions a way to forecast how many intensive-care beds they’d need over the coming fortnight.

Build

From scratch in 20 days

The project was developed from scratch in 20 days, with the help of a couple of friends — a back-end developer and a service designer. The design and code are now open source, and the device has been used in unofficial trials.
