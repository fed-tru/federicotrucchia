Project 06

Arrival Van

An electric commercial van designed to be built in low-CapEx, rapidly scalable microfactories — using proprietary in-house components, materials and software.

Context

Designing systems while the company was built

Arrival’s biggest challenge wasn’t designing vehicles — it was designing entirely new systems while the company itself was still being built. My work focused on interaction systems spanning light, sound, touch, and manufacturing, often finding solutions that improved the user experience while reducing complexity, cost, and production risk.

Case study

Bell push: $40 → $15

One example was the redesign of the capacitive bell push. The original relied on multiple components and expensive manufacturing. By introducing conductive plastics, I simplified the architecture, increased light transmission, and reduced unit cost from roughly $40 to $15.

Lighting

Light as orientation

In parallel I led the exploration of interior lighting for the Van program — investigating how light could support orientation, vehicle-status communication, ingress and egress, and the overall perception of the cabin. I also contributed to AVAS studies and to modular display systems intended to simplify deployment across rapidly scaling facilities.
